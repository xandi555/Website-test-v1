About Me

From early on in my childhood, I was deeply interested in nature — how things are structured, organized, and grow into their existence. I remember watching tall dry grass after a rainfall and being amazed at how it went from lying on the ground, soaking itself, stiffening, and gradually returning to its original glory. This simple observation sparked a lasting interest in how structures behave. Pressure-filled vessels — cells, balloons, tires, and soda cans — have a very different stiffness than most materials used in daily building practice. Yet they offer potential for meeting our growing demands for flexibility, transportation, and environmental adaptation.

During my bachelor studies, I worked extensively with Grasshopper to explore parametric design, modular and fractal structures, and load-bearing optimizations using Karamba3D. Now, in my master’s program, I aim to deepen this mathematical and geometric approach to architecture.

During my three years working in the Kunst2 workshop, I became confident in operating various machines such as CNC mills, laser cutters, and other fabrication tools. This hands-on experience shaped my design approach: I always worked holistically — from concept to fabrication — ensuring that each project was feasible using the available tools and resources.
